Annie may not have her non-profit organization, but she does support more than twenty charities and their causes.

Annie has also been a public supporter of Amnesty International and Greenpeace.

Similarly, in 1999, she donated all of the profits from Eurythmics Peacetour to charities.

And in 2003, Lennox was also a signatory to the No War On Iraq campaign.

Similarly, in 2006, Lennox became a patron of the Master’s Course in Humanitarian and Development Practice for Oxford Brookes University.

In 2009, she led a rally against the Gaza War in London.

Likewise, in 2010, Annie was given the title of UNESCO Goodwill Ambassador for AIDS.

Later that year, in recognition of her humanitarian, she was named an Officer of the Order of the British Empire (OBE).

The singer also received the award in 2011 from Queen Elizabeth II.

Annie has promoted various causes such as Abuse, Adoption, AIDS & HIV, and Civil Rights.

Annie Lennox Career

Lennox started her musical career as a flutist with a band called Dragon’s Playground in 1976.

Later she joined a group called The Tourists, where she was the lead singer from 1977 to 1980.

During this time, she met her fellow musician David Stewart.

After The Tourists disbanded, David and Annie formed their band, the Eurythmics, in 1980 and released their first album, ‘In the Garden’ in 1981.

The duo released its second album, ‘Sweet Dreams (Are Made of This)’ in 1983, which was a commercial breakthrough for the band, which had been struggling for the past two years. 

Likewise, in 1998, after eight years, Annie and her former partner David performed together at a record company party. 

Similarly, in 2007, she released her album Songs of Mass Destruction, which ranked No.7 in the United Kingdom.

Subsequently, in the same year, Annie embarked on a primarily North American tour called ‘Annie Lennox Sing’ from October-November of 2007, which included eighteen stops.
