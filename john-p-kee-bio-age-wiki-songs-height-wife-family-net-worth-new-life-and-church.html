John P. Kee Biography

John P. Kee is an American gospel singer and pastor who was born on June 4, 1962, in Durham, North Carolina, the United States as John Prince Kee. At an early age, P. Kee began developing his musical talent both instrumentally and vocally. He attended the North Carolina School of the Arts in Winston-Salem and at 14 years, P. Kee and his brothers, Wayne and Al, moved to California, where he began attending the Yuba College Conservatory School of Music in Marysville, California.

During this time, P. Kee began playing with various groups such as Cameo and Donald Byrd, and the Blackbyrds. After having a hard time adjusting to California, P. Kee left and moved to Charlotte, North Carolina only to find himself living in a part of the city known for its violence and drug activities.

After watching one of his friends being murdered in a drug deal gone bad, P. Kee rededicated his life back to God during a visitation to a revival meeting.

John P. Kee New Life and Church

John P. Kee officially founded the New Life Fellowship Center where he became and remains the senior pastor. The church is located in the same area where P. Kee had once led a life of crime and drugs. P. Kee says that it was during a trip to Michigan with his choir that Kee received a calling from God to be a preacher.

He was in his mid-twenties when P. Kee became an ordained minister. In 1995, while ministering in Ohio, P. Kee received a prophetic vision and went forward with the building of a Fellowship Center in Charlotte which could minister to the people in the community.
